About water measurements

How river levels are measured

Height is measured from a fixed point close to the river bed. Measurements are collected once or twice a day but more frequently when there's a greater risk of flooding.

Low, normal and high

Typical range is calculated using historic measurements taken at each location. It uses the 5-95 percentiles. Low, normal or high are displayed when the latest level is below, within or above this range.

Rivers levels that are low or below zero

The measuring equipment is fixed but the river bed can change due to errosion or silt deposits. This means its normal for some stations to show measurements that are low or below zero.

Computer models

In most cases a computer model can provide an indication of future levels. However these models have limitations. Where there is significant change you should always refer to flood warnings.
