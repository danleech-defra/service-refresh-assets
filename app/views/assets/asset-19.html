Cookie details

Cookies are small files saved on your phone, tablet or computer when you visit a website.

We use cookies to store anonymised information about how you use the Flood information service so we can improve it based on user needs.

Cookies that measure website use

We use Google Analytics to measure how you use the service. Google does not use or share this information.

Google Analytics stores anonymised information about:

  • how you got to the service
  • the pages you visit on the service and how long you stay on them
  • what you click on while you're using the service

Google Analytics sets the following cookies.

Name What it does Expires
_ga Helps us count how many people visit the service by tracking if you’ve visited before 2 years
_gid Helps us count how many people visit the service by tracking if you’ve visited before 24 hours
_gat Manages the rate at which page view requests are made 10 minutes
_utma Like _ga, this lets us know if you’ve visited before, so we can count how many of our visitors are new to GOV.UK or to a certain page 2 years
_utmb This works with _utmc to calculate the average length of time you spend on the service 30 minutes
_utmc This works with _utmb to calculate when you close your browser When you close your browser
_utmz This tells us how you reached the service (for example from another website or a search engine) 6 months

Strictly necessary cookies

These cookies are essential to making the service work.

We use the following cookies.

Name What it does Expires
seen_cookie_message Stops your browser showing the cookie banner after the first time 1 month
quick-feedback Tells us if you’ve chosen Yes or No to a question so we don’t ask it again 24 hours
TLSVersion Tells us the highest version of TLS your browser uses so we can decide which versions to keep supporting When you close your browser
